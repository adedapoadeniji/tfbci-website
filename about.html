Our Heritage

Over 160 Years of Gospel Witness

The First Baptist Church Ijaye stands as one of the most historic Baptist congregations in Nigeria. Our roots trace back to 1854, when the Gospel first took hold at Ijaye-Orile — in the early days of Baptist mission in south-west Nigeria.

Re-established in 1862 through the sacrificial work of Rev. W. J. Lumbley and the Baptist Foreign Mission Board, the church took root at Ijaye, Abeokuta — and has flourished ever since. Through seasons of growth, challenge, and renewal, the Spirit of God has faithfully led this congregation for over a century and a half.

Today, under the leadership of Rev. Dr. Akinbami Olatubosun, we carry that same apostolic fire — rooted in our heritage, reaching into our future.

Our Pastor

Rev. Dr. Akinbami Oyeniran Olatubosun

Rev. Dr. Akinbami Oyeniran Olatubosun, PhD, is the Senior Pastor of The First Baptist Church Ijaye, Abeokuta. A native of Ogbomoso, Oyo State, he holds a Bachelor of Arts, Master of Arts, and Doctor of Philosophy in Christian Theology — all from the Nigerian Baptist Theological Seminary, Ogbomoso, where he completed his doctoral degree in 2015.

Ordained into full gospel ministry in 2005, Rev. Dr. Olatubosun has served faithfully across several Baptist churches in Ondo, Niger, Oyo, Ogun and Lagos States before answering the call to shepherd this historic congregation in Abeokuta. He brings with him a rich depth of pastoral experience, theological rigour, and a heart for evangelism and community transformation.

He has served in various leadership capacities within the Nigerian Baptist Convention, Ogun Baptist Conference, and the Christian Association of Nigeria (CAN). He is the recipient of multiple awards including the Best Minister of the Year Award (2006), Ambassador of the Bible Society of Nigeria (2012), and the Award of Excellence, CAN Ifelodun LCDA Chapter, Lagos State (2024).

Rev. Dr. Olatubosun is married to Mrs. Felicia Bolanle Olatubosun and they are blessed with children. His life verse and ministry are anchored on the church's mission — sharing Christ's love through meeting the spiritual, emotional, and physical needs of the community.
